THE CHALLENGE

What do we want to accomplish?

Early in 2023, As our customer base grew, we realized that we were starting to see a slew of support tickets related to speed and performance. The amount of support tickets resulted in a big chunk of time and money spent on support, trying to deal with these issues.

Users were saying things like “After running a speed test with my VPN on, I’m getting huge decreases in Upload and Download speeds”. Our task was to find the root cause of these issues and design an elegant solution to help our users and save the company money.


High Level Goals:

  1. Find the root cause of these customer issues
  2. Give our users piece of mind that performance is not an issue
  3. Decrease the number of support tickets revolving around speed and performance to save time and money

My Role:

I led the design team for the VPN product. I worked alongside a Researcher, Content Designer, and Product Manager through the entirety of this project, from conception to launch.

The feature launched globally in 2023 on all platforms.

The approach

EMPATHY

Why is this happening to our users?

Before getting into any type of design discovery, we needed to figure out why this was happening to our users. Other than the fact we were getting these support tickets, we have no idea why this slew of complaints were coming in.


Current behaviours & insights:

We tested asked 13 people about their current experience with VPN.


Recritment criteria:

  • English Only
  • US and Europe
  • An even mixture of male and female
  • A variety of ethnicities
  • An mixture of Android and iOS
  • An mixture of Mobile and Desktop
  • A mixture of Firefox users and Non Firefox users
  • A mixture of existing VPN customers and those who haven’t used the Mozilla VPN

Screen out entirely:

  • Designers, UX researchers, Engineers, or Product Managers
  • Those who have taken more than 2 usertesting tests in the past month
  • Non VPN users
  • A variety of ethnicities
DEFINE

Insights & discovery

We were a bit surprised by the results, an insight that stood out to us was that there were a fair number of people who were unclear about the purpose of switching/selecting servers, and some even worried about using the feature altogether, despite the majority using a VPN everyday.

Choosing a VPN server is not a random act. The distance of the server will affect the latency and performance of your internet speed. After careful thought with our team, we hypothesized that users were not picking servers based on geographical location, thus affecting their speed and performance results negatively.


Verification of discovery

We were able to find more information to support our hypothesis. Our support team had been getting quite a bit of support tickets related to “how to use VPN”. 704 tickets to be exact, in the last year.


“How might we better educate users about how to use VPN so that they are able to easily select the correct server without any performance issues?”
IDEATE

Areas of opportunity


After mapping the current user journey for selecting server locations, we were able to identify 3 main friction points as potential areas of opportunity. These areas were:

  1. Helping the user make the correct decision when they are searching for a new location
  2. Reinforcing that their decision is correct when making the decision to switch
  3. Make sure there are no performance issues after the server selection is made

Target audience: Quick & ambivalent

After consulting our target audience persona and marketing profile, we knew we had to create a solution that would have to educate users in a way that isn’t intrusive, and is within the normal flow of the user journey. Our strategy was to:

  1. Add a list of recommended servers
  2. Add server strength signals beside each location
  3. Design a collapsable educational card to inform the users why the servers are recommended
